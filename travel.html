Travel

There are many options to get into New Delhi International airport but note that you will most likely arrive later at night if you are coming from North America, Africa or Europe. On the way to New Delhi, you will lose a day (i.e. if you depart December 27 evening from the US, you will land in New Delhi the night of December 28. Keeping that in mind, I suggest departing on December 26, so that you make it into town by the night of December 27. For flights out of New Delhi, they will most likely be between the hours of 11 PM-4 AM. Keep that in mind as you finalize dates. I also recommend looking at two one-way tickets as they may be cheaper than a roundtrip.

Visa Information

For citizens of most Western countries, eVisa is okay. Apply 4-30 days prior (should take 2-3 days). I recommend applying 20 days in advance. Please check your nation’s embassy website. If you have any questions, please let me know.

Logistics

  • Getting around: Uber is the best option. The metro is also a good option, depending on where you are going—Janpath is the closest stop. I will most likely have a private taxi or two available for your use on the 28-29. They will be trusted folks who I hire when I am in India. When in India, you should also try the auto-rickshaws, also known as tuk-tuks in some parts of the world.
  • When you land at the airport, I recommend using a government, pre-paid taxi. After you come out from Customs, make a right (towards domestic terminal) and you will see the taxi counter. Tell them where you would like to go, pay, grab the receipt, and head outside to the taxi stand. You can use Uber but in the chaos at New Delhi International, that may be tougher. A government taxi is also the safest option.
  • The currency in India is the Indian rupee ($1 USD = ~68 INR). I recommend withdrawing cash at the airport after you exit Customs. Most of the vendors take Visa or Mastercard but having cash is highly recommended. I keep 3000 INR ($45) on me at any given time.
