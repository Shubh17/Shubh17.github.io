About Us

Time for your favorite Indian couple's engagement! Most importantly, keep in mind that Aditi has no idea about this engagement, so please help me in keeping it that way. Aditi and I met very early on during our first year at Georgia Tech, courtesy of Sam (Healy) Webber. We had very different schedules but we hung out intermittently. We became good friends and stayed that way throughout our time at Tech. We would often go months without hanging out but we easily picked up where we left off. To start the series of clichés that you will also experience as we have our engagement and then wedding, Aditi and I connected over Bollywood movies. You know you’ve found a true friend when you two can bond over the most classic Indian love story (which happens to be 99.9% of movies). Although Aditi and I may not have been best friends in the traditional sense, we both had a high level of comfort with each because of our similar upbringings and interests. After we graduated in 2015, I continued to bother Aditi about her diploma. I needed mine for my Saudi Arabian visa and since we had both graduated at the same time, I wanted to check the status of her diploma. Constant persistence evolved into mutual conversation and eventually all-night FaceTime calls. After months of talking and hanging out intermittently, we both knew. For her birthday, I offered to take her on a trip anywhere she wanted. We eventually ended up going to Montreal, where I asked her to go out with me. At that moment we both knew this was going to be a lifetime commitment. Being great friends with someone before you date them truly changes the trajectory of a relationship. Join us for onw of our many special days ahead!
